Sorry Not Sorry

Empower your Language

Using softening phrases –“I’m sorry”, “Just”, “I think”, “Does that make sense”may seem more polite, however, they undermine your authority, dampen your voice, and discredit your skills and knowledge.

1 – Recognize it!

Did you or your friend use a softening word or phrase? Take notice and find a positive way to restate it

2 – Replace it!

Appreciation over apologies -- Flip the script with “Thank you” instead of “I’m sorry”. Replace the depreciating comment or perception of a negative mistake with a positive statement expressing your gratitude and appreciation. Examples

3 – Reinforce it!

Venmo the young women’s leadership organization @SadieNash a donation with your friend’s @name and what that positive statement was to spread the word to empower and inspire others.

What can I say instead?

I’m sorry for being late.

Thank you for waiting for me.

I’m sorry I am new at this.

I am not familiar with this, can you explain further?

I’m sorry to bother you but…

Hello.

I just wanted to check in on....

Any update on…?

I think I have a plan.

I have a plan.

I actually finished that.

I have finished that.

I’m not an expert but…

I suggest…

I was thinking we could...does that make sense?

I suggest we do...any thoughts or questions?

I’m sorry, thank you for pointing out my mistake.

Thank you for that helpful note!
